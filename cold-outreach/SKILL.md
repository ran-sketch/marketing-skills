# Cold Outreach Skill

## Core Premise

Three problems must be solved before outbound produces reliable results:

1. **How do I contact them?** — Build a list
2. **What do I say?** — Personalize, then deliver big fast value
3. **What if they don't respond?** — Reach out multiple times, across multiple platforms

Solve all three. A great message to a bad list fails. A great list with a weak message fails. Both fail if you only reach out once.

---

## Problem 1 — Build a List

**Goal:** Find the most qualified strangers who could benefit from your offer.

Four sourcing methods — use one or combine:

| Method | Best for | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Software** (Apollo, Clay) | Scale and enrichment | Filter by title, company size, industry; enrich with email/phone |
| **Data brokers** | Fast bulk acquisition | Lower accuracy; verify before sending |
| **Manual search** | Niche or high-value targets | LinkedIn, directories, press releases; slower but precise |
| **Communities** | Warm-adjacent lists | Join groups, forums, Slack channels where your ICP congregates; export members |

**Quality filter:** A smaller list of highly qualified prospects outperforms a massive unqualified one. Define your ICP before building — title, company size, industry, signal (e.g., recently hired, raised a round, posted about a pain you solve).

## Problem 2 — What to Say

Two factors drive strangers to engage: **personalization** and **big fast value**. Both must be present.

### Factor A — Personalization

The goal: make them feel like a friend wrote this, not a marketer.

- Find **1–3 specific facts** about the prospect: a recent post, a company milestone, a shared connection, a podcast they appeared on, a promotion.
- **Compliment it genuinely**, then tie it to how it's relevant to you or people like them.
- This earns 10–30 seconds of attention from someone who's never heard of you.

**Do not:** use generic openers ("I came across your profile"), reference things anyone could have Googled without reading, or use more than one sentence of flattery.

### Factor B — Big Fast Value

- Deliver the biggest, fastest value you can afford — a lead magnet, a specific insight, a free audit, a result you've gotten for someone like them.
- The value must land in under 30 seconds of reading or listening.
- Write scripts and emails at or below a **third-grade reading level**. Run your draft through a readability checker or ask AI to simplify it. Simpler = more replies.
- Phone/chat scripts: **1–2 pages max.** Cold emails: **half a page max.**

For lead magnet and offer design, read `lead-magnets/SKILL.md`.

### Script structure (cold email example)

```
[Personalization line — 1 sentence]
[Bridge — tie the observation to them or their situation]
[Value drop — what you can offer, fast, specific]
[Single CTA — one action, low friction]
```

## Problem 3 — Multi-Platform Follow-Up

One message is not a sequence. Prospects need repeated exposure across multiple channels before they engage.

**Rules:**
- Every sequence should have **multiple touchpoints** (email, LinkedIn, phone, DM) — not just one channel.
- Space touches appropriately: don't blast every channel on day 1, but don't wait weeks between attempts either.
- Each touchpoint should deliver a different angle or piece of value, not just repeat the same message.
- Keep following up until they respond (yes, no, or stop) or until you've exhausted the sequence.

**Sample multi-channel cadence:**
1. Day 1: Cold email
2. Day 2: LinkedIn connection request (no pitch)
3. Day 4: LinkedIn message (personalized, light value)
4. Day 7: Follow-up email (new angle or social proof)
5. Day 10: Phone call
6. Day 14: Final email ("closing the loop")

## Benchmarks — Am I Performing Well?

Use these as baselines. If you're below these, diagnose the bottleneck.

### Phone
| Metric | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| Cold calls per day | 100 |
| Pickup rate | ~20% (20 pickups) |
| Opt-in to lead magnet | ~25% of pickups (5 leads) |
| Viable when | Revenue per customer × conversion rate > 3× cost per rep |

If it takes 4 hours to generate 4–5 engaged leads, you're at ~1 engaged lead/hour. That's workable if your close rate and deal size justify the rep cost.

### Email
| Metric | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| Open rate | ~30% |
| Reply rate (interested) | ~10% of opens → 3% of sent |
| Customers from 100 emails | ~1 (assuming ~33% close rate) |

**Diagnosis guide:**
- Low open rate → Fix subject line or sender reputation
- Low reply rate → Fix personalization or value prop
- Low close rate → Fix offer or qualification

## Evaluation Checklist

When reviewing an outbound approach, check each of these:

**List**
- [ ] Is the ICP clearly defined (title, company size, industry, signal)?
- [ ] Is the list sourced from a reliable method?
- [ ] Is there email/phone verified before sending?

**Message**
- [ ] Does the opening line include 1–3 specific, researched facts?
- [ ] Does the compliment tie to the prospect's world, not just their job title?
- [ ] Is the value offer delivered in under 30 seconds?
- [ ] Is the script at or below a 3rd-grade reading level?
- [ ] Is there a single, low-friction CTA?

**Sequence**
- [ ] Are there touchpoints on at least 2 platforms?
- [ ] Are there at least 4–6 touches in the sequence?
- [ ] Does each touchpoint offer a new angle or piece of value?

**Measurement**
- [ ] Are open rates, reply rates, and conversion rates being tracked?
- [ ] Is the cost-per-engaged-lead being calculated?
- [ ] Is CAC being compared against LTV?

## Why Outbound (Reinforce When Doubted)

When a client questions the channel or asks if it's worth it, reference these advantages:

1. **No content required.** One well-crafted message, refined daily. No ads, no editorial calendar, no brand.
2. **Invisible to competitors.** Private outreach doesn't educate rivals on your acquisition strategy.
3. **Reliable and linear.** More outreach = more leads. No algorithm dependency.
4. **Platform-stable.** Cold email and cold calling rules have barely changed in 30 years. No overnight rule changes.
5. **Compliance-friendly.** Industries banned on ad platforms (cannabis, firearms, financial services) can still do cold outreach.
6. **Makes the business more sellable.** A repeatable outbound engine that doesn't rely on the founder increases business value.
7. **Hard to copy.** List sources, personalization methods, sequencing tools, and messaging are all invisible to competitors.
